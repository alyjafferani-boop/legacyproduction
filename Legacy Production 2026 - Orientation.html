Grade 11–12 Student Theatre Production

The Story of the Aga Khans

Guidance in Changing Times

This production explores key moments from the lives of the Aga Khans and asks one central question: How has guidance responded to changing times?

Central Question
How has guidance responded to changing times?

Every scene should answer this in a different way. The goal is not just to retell history, but to show how guidance responded to the needs of the time.

What This Production Is

This is an annual student-led theatre production where Grade 11 and 12 students work in groups to bring important stories from the lives of the Aga Khans to the stage.

  • Each group presents a 3–4 minute scene
  • Each scene focuses on one event or story
  • Narrators connect the full production across time
Across history, the world has gone through political change, social transformation, crisis, displacement, uncertainty, and renewal. Through these different moments, the Imamat has remained a source of guidance. This production is about understanding how guidance responded in different times and contexts — and what these stories can teach us today.

Production Structure: 10 Scenes

Aga Khan I

1

Receiving the Title of Aga Khan

A moment of leadership, recognition, and the beginning of a new chapter in a changing political world.

Focus: leadership and recognition
2

The Aga Khan Case (1866)

A legal case that brought clarity to community identity and authority during a time of confusion and division.

Focus: identity, authority, and unity

Aga Khan II

3

A Short Imamat, A Steady Hand

A shorter Imamat that still showed continuity, stability, and guidance during a time of transition.

Focus: continuity and stability

Aga Khan III

4

Becoming Imam at Age 8

A young Imam steps into major responsibility while the world around him is changing.

Focus: early responsibility and leadership
5

Guidance During the Bubonic Plague

Leadership, care, and response during a time of fear, illness, and uncertainty.

Focus: care and crisis leadership
6

Supporting Aligarh University

Investment in education and reform to prepare communities for the future.

Focus: education and future-building

Aga Khan IV

7

The Ugandan Expulsion

A story of displacement, resettlement, and guidance during crisis.

Focus: crisis, migration, and rebuilding
8

Improving Quality of Life

A scene showing how real change requires investment across many areas — such as health, education, roads, and development.

Focus: institutions, development, and quality of life
9

Guidance During COVID

Leadership, adaptation, and community care during a global pandemic.

Focus: adaptation and care in a global crisis

Aga Khan V

10

Living the Guidance

Inspired by the marathon on his last birthday, this scene highlights discipline, action, and leading by example.

Focus: practicing what is preached

Performance Format Options

Drama / Short Play

Traditional acting with characters and dialogue.

Spoken Word / Poetry

Expressive storytelling through powerful words and emotion.

News Report / Broadcast

Reporters cover the event as it unfolds.

Interview Format

Characters are interviewed to reveal different perspectives.

Narrative Storytelling

A storyteller guides the audience through the event.

Movement / Physical Theatre

Use body, space, and minimal dialogue to tell the story.

Groups can choose one format or combine two or more formats to create their 3–4 minute scene.

What Each Group Is Responsible For

1

Understand the Story

  • What happened?
  • What was changing at the time?
  • Why did this moment matter?
2

Identify the Message

  • What does this moment show about guidance?
3

Create the Scene

  • 3–4 minutes long
  • Clear structure
  • Simple but effective staging
4

Connect to the Theme

  • Show how your scene answers the central question

Role of Narrators

Narrators are not just there to explain what is happening. They connect scenes, show how times were changing, highlight how guidance responded, and help the audience understand the bigger picture. They are the thread that ties the full production together.

Guidelines for Strong Scenes

Strong scenes should

  • Focus on one clear event or story
  • Show people, emotions, and stakes
  • Be easy for the audience to follow
  • Clearly connect to the larger theme

Avoid

  • Too many historical details
  • Long explanations
  • Trying to show everything at once
  • Turning the scene into a history presentation

Important Reminders

  • Do your research — do not guess the story
  • Plan props and staging early
  • Revise your script after rehearsal
  • Narrators need separate and shared rehearsal time
  • Be prepared — each group affects the whole production
